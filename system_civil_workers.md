### 1. Renting an Apartment with an “Office” – Deduction Rules (Häusliches Arbeitszimmer)
**Strict condition (BMF LStH 2026, §4 Abs. 5 Satz 1 Nr. 6b & §9 Abs. 5 EStG)**:  
A dedicated **häusliches Arbeitszimmer** (home office room) is deductible **only** if it forms the **Mittelpunkt der gesamten betrieblichen und beruflichen Betätigung** (center of all your professional activity). This means >50% of your total working time and core tasks (e.g., planning, admin, creative work) happen there, with no primary external workplace dominating.

- **Most employees** (including most teachers) **do NOT qualify** — school/office is the Mittelpunkt → no actual rent costs deductible.
- **Qualifies** for: Freelancers, consultants, writers, or rare employees where home is truly central (e.g., no employer desk + majority work at home).

**If qualified (Mittelpunkt)**:  
- **Unlimited actual costs** (best option usually) OR choose **Jahrespauschale 1,260 €** (pro-rated by full months; e.g., 945 € for 9 months).  
  Cannot combine with the daily Homeoffice-Pauschale for the same periods.  
- **Deductible costs in a rented apartment (Mietwohnung)**:  
  - Proportional **rent + Nebenkosten** (heating, electricity, water, internet, waste, property tax if passed on).  
    Formula: (Office m² ÷ Total apartment m²) × total monthly rent/Nebenkosten.  
    Example (BMF-aligned): 15 m² office in 80 m² apartment (18.75%) at 1,000 € warm rent/month = **187.50 €/month deductible** (2,250 €/year).  
  - 100% for office-specific items (wallpaper, carpet, lamps, curtains).  
  - Furniture/equipment: Full deduction if ≤952 € (GWG); otherwise depreciate (e.g., desk over 13 years).  
  - Cleaning, repairs to the room.  

**If NOT qualified (vast majority of cases)**:  
- Only the **Homeoffice-Pauschale** (Tagespauschale): **6 € per full workday** worked predominantly at home (max 210 days / 1,260 €/year).  
  - No dedicated room or rent proportion needed — even kitchen table qualifies.  
  - Covers extra electricity/heating/space costs.  
  - Can combine with Pendlerpauschale on mixed days.  
  - Counts toward the 1,230 € Werbungskosten-Pauschale (only extra if total >1,230 €).

**Shared apartment with GF/partner (unmarried, not joint filing)**:  
- Separate tax assessments (each files individually).  
- **You can only deduct costs YOU actually bear/pay**.  
- If shared rental contract: Divide the proportional share (e.g., 50/50 split of rent or per usage agreement).  
- If dedicated office room is **exclusively yours** (no private use by GF, no bed/TV): You claim the **full proportional rent** for that room (based on m²).  
- If GF also works from home: Each claims their own share/room separately (cannot double-claim the same space). One cannot “invoice” the other easily without creating rental income (taxable).  
- If you pay 100% rent (common in unmarried couples): You claim the full proportional amount for your office.  
- Proof: Rent contract + floor plan + bank statements showing your payments.  
- Sources confirm: “If the rental contract is shared … the deductible portion must be divided accordingly” (Accountable); “Each can claim only the costs they bear” (VLH). No joint claim unless married (Zusammenveranlagung).

### 2. How the Tax System Works for Civil Servants/Teachers (Beamte/Lehrer) + System Features
**Core**: Teachers (whether angestellte or Beamte/civil servants) are **normal employees** (nichtselbständige Arbeit, Anlage N). Same EStG rules, same tariff (§32a), same filing process as any wage earner. No special “civil servant tax class”.  

**Key differences/benefits** (all as Werbungskosten/Sonderausgaben):  
- **Pendlerpauschale 2026**: 0.38 € per km (one-way, shortest route) **from the first kilometre** (big upgrade — e.g., 20 km one-way, 220 days = ~1,672 € deductible). School is “erste Tätigkeitsstätte”.  
- **Home office**: Usually only **6 €/day pauschale** (max 1,260 €) because school is Mittelpunkt. However, **school classroom or shared Lehrerzimmer table does NOT count** as “another workplace” for many teachers — so pauschale applies for prep/grading days at home. Full Arbeitszimmer rare (only if home is truly central).  
- **Teaching materials & tools** (huge for teachers):  
  - Books, worksheets, laminating foil, printers, laptops, tablets, software — 100% deductible (even if used at school).  
  - Digital tools >952 €: Full expense or 12-month depreciation.  
  - Internet/phone: Proportional estimate OK.  
- **Double household (Doppelte Haushaltsführung)**: If you maintain a second home near school (e.g., during training or distant posting): Up to **1,000 €/month** for second accommodation + meals (first 3 months) + weekly family home trips (0.38 €/km).  
- **Other teacher-specific**: Class trips (as Dienstreisen: km + Verpflegung), further training/seminar costs, union/professional association fees, moving costs for job.  
- **Beamte extras**: Private Kranken-/Pflegeversicherung contributions fully as Sonderausgaben (no employer share like normal employees). Pension is handled differently but not in income tax return. No “Mindestvorsorgepauschale” issues in 2026 withholding.  
- **Filing**: Voluntary for most (one employer), but always file for refunds (teachers average high Werbungskosten from materials + commute). Lohnsteuerbescheinigung comes automatically.

**Real user impact** (consistent across VLH/Steuerring): Teachers often get 800–2,000 €+ refunds mainly from 2026 Pendler upgrade + materials + home pauschale.
